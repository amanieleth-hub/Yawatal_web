The problem

Every ride app shows you a fare. None of them show you a profit.

Drivers juggling several platforms end up guessing which requests are worth the fuel and time — often deciding in the two or three seconds before a request expires.

Fuel isn't free

A long pickup or a short paid trip can quietly cost more in fuel than it earns.

Seconds to decide

Trip requests expire fast — there's no time to mentally run the math on distance, time, and fare.

Six different apps

Each platform prices differently, so "a good fare" on one app isn't the same number on another.

How it works

Three steps, and it's already done by the time you look.

A trip request appears on your driver app

Yawatal runs quietly alongside Yango Pro, RIDE, Feres, ZayRide, Safe, or Seregela — you keep using the app exactly as before.

Yawatal reads the fare, distance, and duration already on screen

Using Android's Accessibility Service, it reads the text and colors already visible on your screen — nothing is typed or tapped on your behalf.

A verdict appears in a small floating overlay

GREEN, YELLOW, or RED, worked out from ETB per minute and per km after an estimated fuel cost — so you glance once and decide.

The verdict system

One glance. Three colors. No guessing.

Green

Solidly profitable after fuel. Worth taking without a second thought.

Yellow

Marginal. Fine if you're heading that way anyway, worth a pause otherwise.

Red

Likely to cost you more in time and fuel than it pays. Yawatal flags it before you accept.
